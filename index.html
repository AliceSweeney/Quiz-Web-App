AP World History: Period 1 China and Europe Flashcards

China

Government

What type of government did China have? It wasn't a democracy China was ruled by dynasties and emperors that had strong centralized power.

Name two methods China's emperors used to stay in power. Through education and religion Confucianism, because it taught citizens to always follow their emperor, and the Mandate of Heaven, which gave them divine right to rule.

Briefely explain the tribute system. It showed subordination A ritual that all foreigners that interacted with the government would have to perform. It consisted of lots of bowing and offering a gift to the emperor in a show of subordination.

How did the Civil Service exams affect China's government? People got educated Government jobs were based on the score someone got on the test, so better educated people would get better government jobs. This made the government run better because the people in it were more qualified.

Mahayana is the one that gets to China and Japan Therevada Buddhism is more like a philosophy and does not talk about an afterlife or portray the Buddha as a God, it spread to South East Asia. Mahayana Buddhism is more like a religion and does talk about an afterlife and god. It spread to China and Japan.

One believes in Heaven and it is the one that sounds like it does Pureland Buddhism is a religion where the Pureland is a Buddha's space and is roughly equivalent to Heaven. Zen Buddhism focuses on discovering the true meaning of the world through meditation, ignoring language or rational thought.

Economy

Name three goods China manufactured a lot of. One of them is named China bc they made it so well Silk, iron, and porcelain.

How did the Grand Canal affect trade? It's easier to move stuff on water The Grand Canal made it easier to transport goods, so costs went down. This promoted more trade between different regions in China.

What was Champa rice? It's better than normal rice Champa rice was a gift given to China. It is fast ripening and drought resistant, and in some places 2-3 crops could be harvested per season.
