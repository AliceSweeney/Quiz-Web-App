AP World History: Period 1 China and Europe Flashcards

China

Government

What type of government did China have? China was ruled by dynasties and emperors that had strong centralized power.

Name two methods China's emperors used to stay in power. Confucianism, because it taught citizens to always follow their emperor, and the Mandate of Heaven, which gave them divine right to rule.

Briefely explain the tribute system. A ritual that all foreigners that interacted with the government would have to perform. It consisted of lots of bowing and offering a gift to the emperor in a show of subordination.

How did the Civil Service exams affect China's government? Government jobs were based on the score someone got on the test, so better educated people would get better government jobs. This made the government run better because the people in it were more qualified.

Culture

According to Confucianism, what are traits leaders should have? They should lead by example, be worthy of respect, be kind, and overall be a good leader.

According to Confucianism, what are traits followers should have? They should always follow and respect the leader.

What is the difference between Theravada and Mahayana Buddhism? Therevada Buddhism is more like a philosophy and does not talk about an afterlife or portray the Buddha as a God, it spread to South East Asia. Mahayana Buddhism is more like a religion and does talk about an afterlife and god. It spread to China and Japan.

What is the difference between Pureland and Zen Buddhism? Pureland Buddhism is a religion where the Pureland is a Buddha's space and is roughly equivalent to Heaven. Zen Buddhism focuses on discovering the true meaning of the world through meditation, ignoring language or rational thought.

Economy

Name three goods China manufactured a lot of. Silk, iron, and porcelain.

How did the Grand Canal affect trade? The Grand Canal made it easier to transport goods, so costs went down. This promoted more trade between different regions in China.

What was Champa rice? Champa rice was a gift given to China. It is fast ripening and drought resistant, and in some places 2-3 crops could be harvested per season.

Europe

Government

Why was it hard to unify Europe after the Western Roman Empire fell? The geography of Europe is very divisive as it has many mountains, rivers, and islands. There are also many invasions by Germanic and Muslim people.

What is Feudalism? A type of government that arises out of chaos. There may be a King but they are generally weak and must share power with local lords that have their own armies.

What is Manorism? A part of Feudalism. Manors are places vassals will live and community springs up around them. They have serfs and free peasants to farm mostly, but a church or other building could be there too.

Culture

What was the dominant religion and who led it? The Catholic church was the dominant reliogion and was led by the Pope

What were the three other religions in Europe at the time? Judaism, Islam, and some pagan animist or polytheistic religions.

Why did the Crusades happen? Because the Seljuk Turks took over Jerusalem and made it hard for Christians to make their pilgramages, so the Pope wanted to take it back.

Economy

Why was Venice important? It was a dominant trade city on the Medditeranean

Were serfs slaves? Not quite. They didn't belong to their lords and could own things of their own as well as live with their families, but they had to work for the lord and they could not leave the manor without permission.

Did they have agriculture? Yes, and it was mainly done on manors by coerced laborers like serfs.
